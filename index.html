Open Letter

To You. Yes, You.

I know you are tired.

Not the kind of tired that sleep fixes. The kind that has settled into your bones from years of watching the world perform itself at you. From years of being handed two choices and told you are free. From years of feeling something is deeply, fundamentally wrong — and being made to feel foolish for feeling it.

You are not foolish. You are paying attention. And in this world, that is an act of courage most people have quietly stopped performing.

So let me speak plainly, the way no one on television ever will.

The system is not broken. It is working exactly as designed — for the people who designed it. The division you feel between you and your neighbor is not organic. It was cultivated, seeded, watered daily through every screen in your home. Someone studied how you think, mapped the shape of your fears, and built an industry out of making sure you never look up long enough to see who is profiting from your rage.

They do not need to rig the vote. They rig the choice. They decide who you are allowed to consider. They fund the campaigns, own the networks, write the rules for who gets to stand on the debate stage, draw the maps that make your voice matter or disappear — and then they hand you a ballot and call it democracy. The game is decided in rooms you will never be invited into, by people whose names you will never see trend, at gatherings that refuse the presence of cameras and journalists and anyone who might ask an honest question.

And somewhere on an island that sat in plain sight for decades, the most powerful people in the world made themselves untouchable. Evidence gathered. Witnesses silenced. The law found reasons to look the other way. Children were not protected because the people who were supposed to protect them were too busy protecting each other.

That is not a conspiracy theory. That is a pattern. And patterns can be named.

Your faith was weaponized too. Not by God — by the people who decided they would speak for Him. Who built institutions around devotion and used them to demand obedience. Who confused the sacred with the controllable and handed you a religion shaped like a lock. Real faith does not require your silence. Real faith does not punish your questions. Real faith does not need you afraid to survive.

And your children — your children are being reached right now, through the same devices sitting on your kitchen table, by networks the government has called terrorism, that most parents have never heard of, because the people who should be sounding the alarm are too distracted sounding other ones.

I am not telling you these things to make you despair. Despair is the other product they sell.

I am telling you because you already knew. Some part of you has known for a long time. You felt it in the pit of your stomach when you watched the news and realized you were being aimed at someone. You felt it when you voted and nothing changed. You felt it when the names on the flight logs were never spoken aloud in any courtroom that mattered. You felt it when your child came home from school and something behind their eyes was different and you did not know what to say.

That feeling is not weakness. It is conscience. It is the last honest signal in a system built to produce noise.

Here is what they are most afraid of:

Not protests. Not petitions. Not outrage shared and re-shared and clicked into an algorithm that profits from the clicking. They are afraid of something far quieter and far more dangerous. They are afraid of the moment you turn to the person beside you — the one you were told is your enemy — and say: I think we have both been lied to. I think we have been standing in the same fire and blaming each other for the smoke.

That moment. That conversation. That is the one they have spent billions of dollars, decades of infrastructure, and every tool of manufactured division trying to prevent.

So have it.

Talk to your neighbor. Read the actual documents. Ask who paid for what you are watching. Learn the names of the systems that move the money. Protect your children with the same ferocity you feel when you read what is being done to them in the dark. Demand the records. Name the institutions. Refuse to be sold an enemy when the real one is sitting in a room you were never meant to know about.

And do all of it — every single act of clear-eyed, documented, relentless resistance — not from hatred. Hatred is their fuel. Do it from love. The stubborn, unglamorous, ordinary love of someone who refuses to let the world be less than it promised to be.

You were not born to serve a system. You were born to build something worth living in. And it is not too late. It has never been too late as long as there are people willing to wake up and stay awake and reach for the hand of the person standing next to them in the dark.

That is all this is. A hand reaching through the noise.

Take it or leave it. But know that it was offered.

I love you.

Lazarus Grimm
